Privacy Policy for Brownie Extension

Last Updated:

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how the Brownie browser extension ("we", "our", or "the extension") handles information when you use our extension. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about our data practices.

2. Information We Collect

The Brownie extension is designed with privacy in mind. We collect minimal information necessary for the extension to function:

2.1 Local Storage Data

The extension stores the following data locally on your device using IndexedDB:

  • Network Request Data: When using the DevTools panel feature, the extension stores network request information including URLs, HTTP methods, request/response headers, status codes, and response bodies. This data is only collected when you have Chrome DevTools open and is used solely for displaying network requests in the DevTools panel.
  • Tab Information: For the DevTools panel, the extension stores basic tab information (URL and title) to associate network requests with specific tabs. This information is used only for organizing network requests in the DevTools interface.

2.2 Tab Information (Temporary Access)

When you click the extension icon on an arXiv page, the extension temporarily accesses:

  • The current tab's URL (to identify arXiv paper pages)
  • The current tab's title (to generate appropriate PDF filenames)

This information is accessed only when you explicitly click the extension icon and is used immediately to construct the PDF download URL. It is not stored, logged, or transmitted anywhere.

3. How We Use Information

The extension uses collected information for the following purposes:

  • PDF Downloads: To download PDF files from arXiv.org and alphaxiv.org with properly formatted filenames based on paper titles.
  • DevTools Panel: To display network request information in the custom DevTools panel for debugging and development purposes.

4. Data Storage and Retention

All data collected by the extension is stored exclusively on your device using the browser's IndexedDB storage system. We do not transmit, upload, or sync any data to external servers.

  • Network Request Data: Stored locally and automatically cleaned up after 24 hours of inactivity. You can manually clear this data at any time through the DevTools panel interface.
  • Tab Information: Stored locally and automatically cleaned up when tabs are closed or after 24 hours of inactivity.

5. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not share, sell, or transmit any data to third parties. All data remains on your device and is never sent to external servers, analytics services, or any other third-party services.

The extension only accesses external websites (arXiv.org and alphaxiv.org) to download PDF files when you explicitly initiate a download. These downloads are performed directly in your browser, and no information about your downloads is transmitted to us or any third party.

6. Permissions Explanation

The extension requests the following permissions:

  • Host Permissions (https://*/*): Required to download PDF files from arXiv.org and alphaxiv.org. The extension only accesses these specific domains when you initiate a download.
  • Storage: Required to store network request data locally for the DevTools panel feature.
  • DevTools: Required to create a custom DevTools panel for network request monitoring.
  • Tabs: Required to read the current tab's URL and title when you click the extension icon to download PDFs.
  • ActiveTab: Required to communicate with the content script in the active tab to initiate PDF downloads.

7. Your Rights and Control

You have full control over your data:

  • You can clear all stored network request data at any time through the DevTools panel interface.
  • You can uninstall the extension at any time, which will remove all locally stored data.
  • You can disable the extension without uninstalling it, which will prevent any data collection.

8. Security

We take data security seriously. All data is stored locally on your device using the browser's built-in secure storage mechanisms. We do not transmit any data over the network, which eliminates the risk of data interception or unauthorized access during transmission.

9. Children's Privacy

The extension is not intended for use by children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can delete such information.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.
